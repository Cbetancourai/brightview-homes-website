★ Clermont, FL overview

About building new in Clermont

Clermont sits on the highest terrain in peninsular Florida — actual rolling hills, with the iconic Citrus Tower at one of the highest points. The city has grown from 9,000 residents in 2000 to over 45,000 today, driven by proximity to the Orlando theme parks, a booming restaurant scene, and the Clermont Chain of Lakes. Scattered-lot opportunities exist across the city, with Brightview homes typically landing in the $400K–$600K range.

★ Why build here

Why buyers are choosing Clermont

  • Clermont Chain of Lakes — 14 connected lakes for boating, bass fishing, and waterfront living.
  • Rolling hills, citrus history, and Florida's legitimate 'hill country' topography.
  • 20 minutes to Walt Disney World via US-27 and SR-429.
  • National Training Center — Olympic-caliber fitness center that put Clermont on the triathlon map.
  • Growing downtown, new Publix-anchored centers, and Waterfront Park on Lake Minneola.

★ Location & commute

Getting around from Clermont

Clermont sits at the intersection of the Florida Turnpike and US-27. Walt Disney World is 20 minutes south via SR-429, Orlando International Airport is 45 minutes via the Turnpike, and downtown Orlando is 35 minutes via SR-50.

Schools

Lake County Schools — Cypress Ridge Elementary, East Ridge Middle, and East Ridge High School serve most Clermont residents. Montverde Academy is a nationally ranked private school option in the area.
